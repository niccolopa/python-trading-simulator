# python-trading-simulator

**From Retail Trading to Python: My First Vectorial Backtester**
Hello! I am Niccolò, a first-year BSc student in Economics and Finance with Data Science.
Before starting university I was a retail trader with some prop firms (swing and intraday). Instead of waiting for advanced programming courses to start, I decided to teach myself Python to answer a question I'd been asking myself for a while: "_Do my discretionary graphical strategies really work when tested mathematically on 10 years of historical data?_"
This repository contains my very first Python project: a hybrid (vectorial + event-driven) backtest engine built entirely from scratch with pandas and numpy to test a "Pullback" logic on the S&P 500 index.



**The Idea: Quantifying the "Golden Zone**
As a human trader, I was always looking for Fibonacci retracements (area 0.5 - 0.618) in a defined trend, confirmed by volumes. Teaching a computer to track Fibonacci is complex for a first project, so I mathematically approximated my strategy using objective indicators:
1) The Trend: The 20-period EMA must be above the 50-period EMA (Long-only filter, given that the S&P 500 is a structurally bullish market).
2) The Pullback (the Golden Zone): The closing price drops below EMA 20, but bounces off holding EMA 50 as support.
3) The Confirmation (Volumes): The volume of the day must be above its 20-day moving average (detection of institutional activity).
4) The Momentum: The 14-period RSI must be in a neutral area (40-60), indicating healthy consolidation and not overbought.



**The Architecture of the Code**
The code is divided into two main logics to simulate the market as realistically as possible and avoid "cheating" by looking into the future:
- Signal Generation (Vectorized): Use the fast functions of _pandas_ and _numpy_ to scan 10 years of history and find the days when all 4 of my rules align.
- Trade Management (Event-Driven Loop): This was the hardest part to program. I wrote a loop that handles Risk/Reward exactly as I would do by hand: 1 to 2. The trade enters at the opening the day after the signal and closes rigidly only if it touches a Take Profit of +4% or a Stop Loss of -2%.



**The Results (2016-2026)**
- Operations Exguite: 43
- Win rate: 44.19%
- Cumulative Return (Strategy): +29.74%
- Cumulative Return (S&P 500 Buy & Hold): ~+200%



**What I learned**
At first glance, doing 30% in 10 years against 200% for the S&P 500 seems like a defeat. But analyzing the data, I understood market dynamics that I would never have noticed just by looking at a candlestick chart
1) The algorithm works (Mathematically): A win rate of 44% with an R/R of 1:2 has a positive mathematical expectation. When the system enters the market, it statistically gains.
2) Capital Protection: Looking at the graph generated by my code, my strategy line remains completely flat during the bloody crashes of 2020 (Covid) and 2022. The system was in liquidity because there were no secure setups. He protected capital brilliantly.
3) The problem of underperformance: Resounding underperformance comes from frequency. The algorithm is so selective that in 10 years it has done only 43 operations. For 80% of the decade, my capital has remained stagnant without generating interest, losing the physiological bullish momentum of the index. So, I realized that in algorithmic trading, it's not enough to have a good strategy, you have to optimize the use of capital.
